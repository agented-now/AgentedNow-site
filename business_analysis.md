## 1. Ideal Client Profiling, Outreach & Contact Strategy

### 1.1 Ideal Client Profiles (ICPs)

#### ICP A — Mid-Stage US Startups (Series B–D, 100–500 employees)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Industry** | B2B SaaS, DevTools, FinTech, HealthTech, Cybersecurity |
| **Revenue** | $10M–$100M ARR |
| **Pain** | Engineering teams stretched thin; need internal tooling but can't justify hiring a full team for it. Off-the-shelf tools don't fit their workflows. |
| **Budget** | $50–100K per project — fits their "build vs. buy" sweet spot |
| **Champion** | CTO, VP Engineering, sometimes VP Product |
| **Signal** | Recently raised a round, hiring for AI/ML roles, posting about "AI transformation" on LinkedIn, using LLMs in product but not yet internally |
| **Geography** | US (primary), UK, Western Europe (secondary) |

**Why they buy:** They move fast, have budget, and understand that a custom agentic tool for their engineers or analysts pays for itself in weeks. They've tried ChatGPT/Copilot but need something deeply integrated with their data and workflows.

---

#### ICP B — Growth-Stage Israeli Tech Companies (50–300 employees)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Industry** | Cyber, homeland security, defense-adjacent, enterprise SaaS |
| **Pain** | Sensitive data environments (air-gapped, on-prem, compliance-heavy). Off-the-shelf AI products are a non-starter. Need open-source models, strict isolation. |
| **Budget** | $20–50K per project |
| **Champion** | CTO, VP R&D |
| **Signal** | Government/defense contracts, SOC2/ISO compliance, mentions of "sovereign AI" or "on-prem LLM" |
| **Geography** | Israel |

**Why they buy:** Security and IP constraints eliminate 90% of vendors. Custom agentic apps on open-source models, running on their infra, is exactly what they need — and very few shops can deliver it.

---

#### ICP C — US Mid-Market / Enterprise Innovation Teams (500–5,000 employees)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Industry** | Financial services, insurance, logistics, manufacturing, professional services |
| **Pain** | Business analysts and ops teams drown in data. Existing BI tools are too rigid. They want "conversational BI" or intelligent internal assistants but can't get there with Copilot plugins. |
| **Budget** | $50–150K per project (larger orgs, longer engagements) |
| **Champion** | VP Engineering, Head of Data/AI, CTO, sometimes CDO |
| **Signal** | "AI Center of Excellence" initiatives, hiring prompt engineers, recent AI strategy announcements, failed POCs with generic tools |
| **Geography** | US |

**Why they buy:** They've tried the big platforms (Microsoft, Google, Salesforce AI) and hit walls on customization. They need something built around *their* data, *their* terminology, *their* workflows — not a one-size-fits-all chatbot.

---

#### ICP D — Venture-Backed AI-Native Companies (Seed–Series A, 10–50 employees)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Industry** | AI-first startups building products that need agentic capabilities at their core |
| **Pain** | Small team, huge ambition. Need complex agentic architecture (LangGraph, multi-agent, tool-use) but don't have the in-house depth. |
| **Budget** | $20–50K (smaller scope, faster turnaround) |
| **Champion** | CEO/CTO (often the same person) |
| **Signal** | AI-focused pitch decks, YC/TechStars alumni, building "AI copilot for X" |
| **Geography** | US, Israel |

**Why they buy:** They need a seasoned agentic-AI team to build their core product or a critical internal tool, fast. Vibe coding means they get something working in days, not months.

### 1.2 How to Find & Contact Them

#### Channels & Tactics

| Channel | Tactic | Target ICP |
|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn (outbound)** | Targeted connection requests + personalized DMs to CTOs/VPs Eng at companies matching ICP signals. Use Sales Navigator filters: company size, industry, recent funding, job postings for AI roles. | A, B, C, D |
| **LinkedIn (content)** | Weekly posts showing real project outcomes (anonymized): "We built an agentic BI tool that let business owners skip the analyst queue — here's what we learned." Build authority. | All |
| **Warm intros (Israel)** | Leverage existing Israeli client network. Ask for referrals. Israeli tech is tight-knit — one good intro opens doors. | B |
| **Warm intros (US)** | Ask Israeli clients with US HQs or US investors to intro you to their portfolio/network. | A, D |
| **Clutch / Toptal / similar** | Create a polished profile showcasing agentic AI expertise. Many US companies search these platforms for specialized dev shops. | A, C |
| **YC / accelerator networks** | Engage with YC Slack/forums, offer "office hours" or free 30-min agentic architecture reviews. | D |
| **Conferences & meetups** | Attend/speak at AI Engineer Summit, LangChain meetups, local AI meetups in Tel Aviv and SF/NYC. Even virtual talks build pipeline. | All |
| **Cold email** | Targeted sequences to CTOs at companies showing ICP signals. Keep it short, lead with a relevant case study, offer a free 30-min architecture session. | A, C |
| **Content marketing (blog/site)** | Publish case studies, "agentic AI architecture patterns" posts, comparisons (custom vs. off-the-shelf). SEO for "custom AI agent development", "LangGraph consulting", "agentic SaaS development". | All |
| **Partnerships** | Partner with LangChain/LangSmith, cloud providers (AWS, Azure, GCP) — get listed as a recommended implementation partner. | A, C |

### 3.2 Website Structure & Page-by-Page Instructions

#### Page 1: Home (Landing Page)

**Sections (top to bottom):**

1. **Hero**
   - Tagline (large, bold)
   - 1–2 sentence value prop
   - CTA button: "Book a Free Architecture Session" → Calendly link
   - Subtle animated background (abstract nodes/connections — agentic feel, not cheesy)

   - Left: "The problem" — off-the-shelf AI tools are generic, inflexible, insecure
   - Right: "Our approach" — custom, end-to-end, secure, deeply integrated
   - Use icons or minimal illustrations

3. **What We Build** (3 cards)
   - **Agentic Internal Tools** — intelligent assistants for engineering, ops, research
   - **Conversational BI** — business owners talk to their data, get interactive dashboards
   - **AI-Powered Workflows** — automations + agents for sales, marketing, support

   1. Define & Plan — product definition, benchmarks, metrics
   2. POC → MVP — start fast with vibe coding, iterate
   3. Build & Integrate — full agentic architecture, UI, integrations
   4. Deploy & Support — production deployment, monitoring, iteration

5. **Case Studies** (carousel or grid, 3 items)
   - Anonymized versions of the three projects from README
   - Each: industry tag, problem, solution, outcome (quantified if possible)

6. **Why Custom?** (comparison table or split section)
   - Custom vs. off-the-shelf (AskGuru, generic copilots, etc.)
   - Columns: Customization, Security, Performance, Cost, Speed

7. **Trust & Security**
   - Open-source models, on-prem deployment, IP protection
   - Compliance-ready (SOC2, air-gapped environments)
   - "Your data never leaves your environment"

8. **CTA / Contact**
   - "Let's build your AI." + contact form or Calendly embed
   - Email, LinkedIn link

9. **Footer**
   - Logo, tagline, links, social icons, copyright

- Expanded versions of the three projects

  - **Challenge** — what the client needed and why off-the-shelf failed
  - **Solution** — what was built, architecture highlights
  - **Outcome** — impact, speed, user adoption

### 3.3 Design Direction

#### Visual Identity

| Element | Direction |
|---|---|
| **Color palette** | Dark primary (deep navy or charcoal #1A1A2E or #0F0F1A), electric accent (cyan #00D4FF or violet #7C3AED), white/light gray for text and cards. Dark mode feel — premium, techy. |
| **Typography** | Clean sans-serif. Headings: Inter, Space Grotesk, or Satoshi (bold/black weight). Body: Inter or DM Sans (regular/medium). |
| **Logo** | Wordmark + minimal icon. The icon could be abstract (nodes, connections) or the animal if using an animal name. Keep it simple enough for a favicon. |
| **Illustrations** | Abstract, geometric — nodes, connections, flow diagrams. Avoid stock photos of people pointing at screens. If using illustrations, keep them flat/line-art style with the accent color. |
| **Animations** | Subtle. Fade-in on scroll, gentle parallax on hero, animated node connections in background. Nothing flashy — let the content breathe. |
| **Cards & sections** | Glassmorphism or soft shadows on dark backgrounds. Rounded corners (12–16px). Generous whitespace. |

#### Responsive Design

- Mobile-first layout
- Hero: stack vertically, CTA prominent
- Cards: single column on mobile, 2–3 columns on desktop
- Navigation: hamburger menu on mobile, minimal top nav on desktop
- Font sizes: scale down gracefully, maintain readability

#### Tone of Voice

| Attribute | Example |
|---|---|
| **Confident, not arrogant** | "We build AI tools that work" — not "We're the best AI company ever" |
| **Technical, not jargon-heavy** | "Agents that reason, retry, and use tools" — not "leveraging synergistic AI paradigms" |
| **Direct, not salesy** | "Here's what we built. Here's what it did." — not "Unlock the power of AI transformation!" |
| **Human, not corporate** | "We sit with your team and figure out what they actually need" — not "We facilitate stakeholder alignment sessions" |
